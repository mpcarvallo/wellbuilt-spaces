# Product Specification — Validation MVP

## Core promise
“In about seven minutes, identify the three practical home improvements most aligned with your household, goals, budget, and ability.”

## Target first customer
Homeowners and renters who care about wellness and sustainability but feel overwhelmed by conflicting advice and cannot justify an expensive home consultation.

## Job to be done
Help me decide what to improve first, why it matters, what I can do myself, and what can wait.

## MVP flow
Landing → Goals → Household → Home → Capacity → Air/Kitchen → Moisture → Materials → Water → Light/Sleep → Comfort → Sustainability → Maintenance → Starting room → Review → Snapshot → Usefulness rating → Optional email.

## Snapshot hierarchy
1. One-sentence interpretation.
2. Three actions, ranked.
3. For each: why, steps, cost band, time, confidence, professional escalation.
4. “Later, not now” list.
5. Missing information questions.
6. Educational disclaimer.

## Free vs paid test
Free: three priorities and one detailed action.
Paid experiment ($29–$49): all detailed actions, room roadmap, 90-day sequence, printable plan, product-selection criteria, maintenance calendar.

## Success is not questionnaire completion alone
The strongest early metric is “I would do one of these actions in the next seven days.”
